# Kabir sales analysis report 

## 1. Executive Summary
Over the three-year period from 2010 to 2012, I observed a clear and consistent decline in sales performance.
In 2010, total revenue was ₦104,720, which dropped to ₦83,600 in 2011, and then slightly further to ₦80,960 in 2012. So, year after year, the business is making less revenue.
Now, even though revenue is dropping, the business is still active, there were 24 transactions handled by five sales representatives, generating a total of ₦269,280 overall.
What this tells me is that the issue is not really activity… it’s value.
Sales are happening, but they are bringing in less money per transaction, and that’s a major concern.
So, at this point, I’d say management really needs to step in, understand what’s causing this decline, and fix it before moving into 2013.

## 4. Business Recommendations
A. Improve Sales Performance

Instead of adding more people, I would focus on improving the current team.


For example:

## Abdul could benefit from training on:
i. Upselling
ii. Closing higher-value deals
iii. Targeting better clients

B. Focus on Revenue Quality

Right now, it’s too much about quantity.

I would recommend:

•	Setting minimum deal values
•	Encouraging reps to focus on bigger, more profitable sales

C. Review Operations

The decline suggests something deeper.

So, I would suggest:

•	Looking at sales territories
•	Understanding where performance is dropping
•	Reviewing pricing and product positioning

D. Hiring Decision for 2013

Honestly, based on this data, I would not recommend hiring new staff yet.

Here’s why:

•	Revenue is dropping every year
•	Sales volume is also declining
•	Hiring more people will just increase costs without solving the real problem

Instead:
•	Focus on improving current performance
•	Monitor results in early 2013 (Q1–Q2)
•	 Then decide if expansion makes sense

## 5. Conclusion

So overall, what I’m seeing is this:
The business is still active, but it’s becoming less efficient and less profitable over time.
The main focus now shouldn’t be growth or expansion, it should be:

•	Improving performance
•	Increasing deal value
•	Fixing the downward trend
If these issues are addressed properly, the business has a strong chance of bouncing back and performing better in the coming years.
